Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 21, 2026

Life Balance ("the app") is a personal Five Elements tracker. This page explains what happens to your data when you use it.

What data the app collects

The app does not operate its own database and does not use accounts, cookies, analytics, or advertising trackers. Specifically:

  • Journal entries you write are saved only in your own browser's local storage (localStorage) on your device — not uploaded, synced, or sent anywhere.
  • Any photo, video, or audio you attach to a post or story is uploaded directly from your browser to Cloudinary, a third-party media hosting service, and stored there. Only the resulting media address is saved on your device alongside your post; the developer's own server never receives or stores the file.

Third-party resources

Loading the app's pages, and posting media, requests resources from a small number of third parties:

  • Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com) — for the Inter and Fraunces typefaces.
  • Pi Network SDK (sdk.minepi.com) — loaded when the app runs inside the Pi Browser, used to detect that environment.
  • Cloudinary (cloudinary.com) — stores any photo/video/audio you attach to a post or story, as described above. Cloudinary's own privacy policy governs how it processes that file.

These requests are subject to those providers' own privacy policies. The app does not pass your journal text, or any data beyond the attached file itself, to them.

Pi Network integration

The app uses the Pi SDK to detect whether it's running inside Pi Browser, and on one dedicated setup page, to process a Pi payment via Pi.authenticate() and Pi.createPayment(). When you make a payment, the app's server relays only the payment identifier and blockchain transaction ID to Pi Network's own Payments API to approve and complete it — the server does not store this information itself; it is forwarded to Pi Network, whose own privacy policy governs how Pi Network processes it. The app does not otherwise collect, log, or retain any Pi account or payment data.

Your data, your control

Your post text and records always live in your own browser, and you're always in control of them:

  • Delete an individual post any time using the ✕ button on that post — this removes it from your browser immediately.
  • Clear everything at once by clearing this site's data in your browser's settings (e.g. "Clear browsing data" → site data for this domain).
  • Note: deleting a post removes its record from your browser, but the underlying media file on Cloudinary is not automatically deleted at this time. Contact the developer (below) if you need a specific file removed from Cloudinary.

Children's privacy

The app is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect data from anyone, regardless of age — it doesn't collect data from anyone at all, as described above.

Changes to this policy

If how the app handles data changes, this page will be updated and the "Last updated" date above will change accordingly.
