Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 20, 2026

Life Balance ("the app") is a personal Five Elements tracker. This page explains what happens to your data when you use it.

What data the app collects

The app itself does not operate a server and does not collect, transmit, or store any of your data on infrastructure run by the developer. Specifically:

  • Journal entries you write, and any photo, video, or audio you attach to them, are saved only in your own browser's local storage (localStorage) on your device.
  • Nothing you post is uploaded, synced, or sent anywhere — not to the developer, not to other users, not to any cloud service.
  • The app does not use accounts, cookies, analytics, or advertising trackers.

Third-party resources

Loading the app's pages requests a small number of resources from third parties, which is true of essentially any website:

  • Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com) — for the Inter and Fraunces typefaces.
  • Pi Network SDK (sdk.minepi.com) — loaded when the app runs inside the Pi Browser, used to detect that environment.

These requests are subject to those providers' own privacy policies. The app does not pass any of your journal content to them.

Pi Network integration

At this time, the app's use of the Pi SDK is limited to detecting whether it's running inside Pi Browser (sandbox/testnet mode). It does not currently call Pi authentication or payment APIs, so no Pi account data or payment data is collected or processed by the app. If a future version adds sign-in or payments, this policy will be updated before that happens, and Pi Network's own privacy policy would additionally govern any data Pi Network itself processes.

Your data, your control

Since everything lives in your browser, you're always in control of it:

  • Delete an individual post any time using the ✕ button on that post.
  • Clear everything at once by clearing this site's data in your browser's settings (e.g. "Clear browsing data" → site data for this domain).

Children's privacy

The app is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect data from anyone, regardless of age — it doesn't collect data from anyone at all, as described above.

Changes to this policy

If how the app handles data changes, this page will be updated and the "Last updated" date above will change accordingly.
